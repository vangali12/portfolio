About Me!

Chemist turned Coder.

I have a Bachelor of Science in Analytical Chemistry from the University of Washington and worked as an analytical chemist for a little over a year. As a chemist in the pharmaceutical industry, I had the responsibility of optimizing mass spectrometry methods unique to each drug and providing timely and accurate data to clients.

I saw that supporting technology was unable to adapt to these scientific innovations due to a misunderstanding of user needs and specific technical knowledge.

I decided to learn to code.

Devoted to Design.

Now, I'm a full-stack web developer with an eye for beautifully functional design.

In the science community visual representation of complex data seems unimportant or invaluable but good data visualization is paramount to communicating discoveries to a diverse audience.

But design isn't just about appearances. Good design opens the door to new and efficient ways of solving complex challenges. The ultimate objective is to find the intersection of user needs and software accessibility.

So I decided to focus on learning good design.

Guided by Goals

I'm looking for opportunities to build beautifully practical software with the goal of one day building well-designed, user-focused, and intelligent software for chemical instrumentation.
